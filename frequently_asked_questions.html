Who is the program tailored for?

The courses are designed for developmental disability (DD) agency professionals employed in upper and middle management positions who aspire to career progression.

What is the schedule for the program?

The length of the program is 24 weeks with a break over the holidays. The first semester begins on September 14, 2017 and ends December 7, 2017. There are 12 weeks in the first semester and 12 weeks in the second semester, which will begin on February 8, 2018 and end April 26, 2018.

The first three-hour session the week of September 14 will include a program orientation, as well as content, to be held at the Institute for Community Inclusion on the University of Massachusetts Boston (UMB) campus.

There will be 10 on-line training courses following the first session. It is anticipated that each on-line module will take at least three hours to complete. The on-line courses can be completed at the time and place of the student’s choice as long as the course is completed within the designated week. This gives working professionals flexibility in scheduling. There will be a final face to face session in the first semester at UMB. The second semester will follow the same format with a face-to-face session to begin the semester followed by 10 weeks of on-line instruction and then conclude with a final face to face session at UMB.

What are the pre-requisites for the program?

There are no academic requirements for the program.

What is the cost?

The cost for the program is $4,000, plus a book fee of $75-$100 for each semester.

Payment can be made in full once accepted into the program. Otherwise, two separate payments of $2,000 each are allowed. First payment is due on or before August 31, 2017 and second payment is due on or before February 1, 2018.

What are the course titles?

The certificate program will provide the following courses:

  1. Orientation to developmental disabilities,
  2. Mission, vision and values
  3. Organizational culture
  4. Organizational change
  5. Strategic planning
  6. Oral and written communications
  7. Governance
  8. Financial management
  9. Grant writing
  10. DISC leadership assessment
  11. Leadership
  12. Public policy
  13. Marketing and public relations
  14. Coaching and supporting employees
  15. Managing projects and programs
  16. Diversity and cross-cultural understanding
  17. Information technology
  18. Performance management
  19. Independent study

Additionally, content will be supplemented by webinars, special interest group discussions, and discussions corresponding to reading assignments.

How was the curriculum determined?

University of Massachusetts Boston instructional staff conducted a curriculum review of other executive management and leadership programs offered around the country to assure that the DD Agency Leadership and Management certificate curriculum reflected contemporary practices. Furthermore, four focus groups were held in Massachusetts attended by DD provider leaders and upper and middle managers to provide input into the competencies required to lead DD agencies now and into the future.

What is the application process?

Interested students must complete and submit an application form by July 15, 2017. A candidate’s supervisor will be asked to sign the application allowing for students to implement policies and practices within their organizations based on their learning and through course field assignments.

When a student has been accepted into the program, they will be required to submit the fee for the first semester ($2,000) on or before August 31, 2017. Payment for the second semester ($2,000) will be due on or before February 1, 2018. Students will not receive a certificate of completion unless payment is made in full.

Who will teach courses?

Our faculty includes ICI staff, others having expertise in the content area, and professional leaders working within DD provider organizations and/or Massachusetts state agencies.

How do I seek help during the program?

Each student will be assigned a faculty advisor. The advisor is available to answer student questions. The advisor reviews and responds to the course assignments.

What happens if I fail?

Assignments are not graded. Instructors will provide continual support and feedback to students on field assignments until the student receives competency levels.

What are the requirements to receive the certificate?

Participation/attendance at four sessions at UMB and completion of on-line courses, webinars, and field assignments associated with courses are required to receive the certificate. Each student will receive a syllabus that will include learning objectives for each course, description of field assignments, and reading and resource lists.

Are there other requirements?

Students will be required to have access to Internet with either a Windows or Mac operating system. Technical assistance, free of charge, will be provided to students experiencing difficulty with the on-line training platform.

How many credits will I receive?

This is a non-credit program.

How does this program differ from other leadership and management training programs?

The DD Agency Leadership and Management certificate program has been developed specifically for individuals who aspire to be in leadership positions within DD organizations. The certificate program is unique for our business, which will better position graduates for leadership and management careers in the field of developmental disabilities.
