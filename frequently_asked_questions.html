Frequently Asked Questions

Who is the program tailored for?

The courses are designed for developmental disability (DD) agency professionals employed in upper and middle management positions who aspire to career progression.

What is the schedule for the program?

The length of the program is 24 weeks with a break over the holidays. The first semester begins the week of September 12, 2016 and ends the week of December 5, 2016. There are 12 weeks in the first semester and 12 weeks in the second semester, which will begin the week of February 13, 2017 and end the week of May 8, 2017.

The first three-hour session the week of September 12 will include a program orientation, as well as content, to be held on the University of Massachusetts Boston (UMB) campus.

There will be 10 on-line training courses following the first session. It is anticipated that each on-line module will take at least three hours to complete. The on-line courses can be completed at the time and place of the student's choice as long as the course is completed within the designated week. This gives working professionals flexibility in scheduling. There will be a final session in the first semester at UMB. The second semester will follow the same format with a face-to-face session to begin the semester followed by 10 weeks of on-line instruction and then conclude with a final session at UMB.

What are the pre-requisites for the program?

There are no academic requirements for the program.

What is the cost?

The cost for the program is $3,500, plus a book fee of $200-300 for each semester.

Payment can be made in full once accepted into the program. Otherwise, two separate payments of $1,700 each are allowed. First payment is due on or before August 31, 2016 and second payment is due on or before February 1, 2017.

Payment:

Payment will be made through credit card and due when participants register.

What are the course titles?

The certificate program will provide the following courses:

  1. History, mission and values
  2. Legislation and public policy
  3. Organizational culture
  4. Organizational change
  5. Strategic planning
  6. Communications
  7. Financial management
  8. Public policy and advocacy
  9. Leadership
  10. Leadership style inventory
  11. Risk management and legal affairs
  12. Marketing and public relations
  13. Coaching and supporting employees
  14. Managing projects and programs
  15. Diversity and cross-cultural understanding
  16. Information technology
  17. Performance management
  18. Collaboration

How was the curriculum determined?

University of Massachusetts Boston instructional staff conducted a curriculum review of other executive management and leadership programs offered around the country to assure that the DD Agency Leadership and Management certificate curriculum reflected contemporary practices. Furthermore, four focus groups were held in Massachusetts attended by DD provider leaders and upper and middle managers to provide input into the competencies required to lead DD agencies now and into the future.

What is the application process?

Interested students must complete and submit an application form by July 15, 2016. A candidate's supervisor will be asked to sign the application allowing for students to implement policies and practices within their organizations based on their learning and through course field assignments.

When a student has been accepted into the program, they will be required to submit the fee for the first semester ($1,750) on or before August 31, 2016. Payment for the second semester ($1,750) will be due on or before February 1, 2017. Students will not receive a certificate of completion unless payment is made in full.

Who will teach courses?

There will be a combination of academic professors, instructors, and professional leaders serving within DD provider organizations and/or Massachusetts state agencies.

What happens if I fail?

Assignments are not graded. Instructors will provide continual support and feedback to students on field assignments until the student receives competency levels.

What are the requirements to receive the certificate?

Participation/attendance at four sessions at UMB and completion of on-line courses, webinars, and field assignments associated with courses are required to receive the certificate. Each student will receive a syllabus that will include learning objectives for each course, description of field assignments, and reading and resource lists.

Are there other requirements?

Students will be required to have access to Internet with either a Windows or Mac operating system. Technical assistance, free of charge, will be provided to students experiencing difficulty with the on-line training platform.

How many credits will I receive?

This is a non-credit program.

How does this program differ from other leadership and management training programs?

The DD Agency Leadership and Management certificate program has been developed specifically for individuals who aspire to be in leadership positions within DD organizations. The certificate program is unique to the field, which will better position graduates in leadership and management careers in the DD field.
