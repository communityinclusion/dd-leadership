So is there a way for you to not only stay on top of these changes, but also use them to empower your agency?

Our new leadership and management certificate program will get you there.

The School for Global Inclusion and Social Development at UMass Boston and the Association of Developmental Disabilities Providers collaborated to create courses just for you. In fact, this is the only program specifically created for Massachusetts DD providers and leaders.

Our curriculum applies research on executive management training, and integrates actual DD agency scenarios within each course. Instructors include employment experts from the Institute for Community Inclusion, as well as innovative DD managers from across the Commonwealth.

“What leadership has taught me is that one person cannot succeed alone. One person cannot be the leader and operate in isolation.” Gary Blumenthal, President & CEO, Association of Developmental Disabilities Providers

Don’t get left behind. Lead the change to create the best results for your agency, your staff, and the individuals you serve.

This hybrid course is a combination of in-person and online instruction. You’ll value both the convenience of the online format, and the personal connections built through our sessions on the UMass Boston campus.

After completing the two 12-week sessions, you’ll get a non-credit certificate in contemporary practices in developmental disabilities leadership and management from UMass Boston.

This course is rigorous and rewarding, and you’ll start putting what you learn into practice immediately.

Leadership and Management Transformation Certificate Program

Executive Leadership for ADDP Member Organizations

Syllabus: Each course instructor will be responsible for developing a syllabus and basic competencies.

Course assignments will be designed to improve an individual, team or organizational policy or practice.

Certificate: Upon completion of all courses and assignments, students will receive a non-credit Certificate in Developmental Disabilities Leadership and Management from the School of Global Inclusion and Social Development at the University of Massachusetts, Boston.

Format: A (Hybrid) combination of in person and online education.

Physical Location: UMass Boston campus

Semester 1: Leadership 12 Weeks, begins September 12, 2016
Week(s) 1 & 2: Orientation to Certificate Program; History, Values, Legislation and Practices in the Field of Developmental Disabilities Reviewing history of developmental disability field from institutional care to community services and supports; values, key legislation, prevalence, civil rights, state and local systems
Week(s) 3 & 4: and Vision, Values, Mission Creating, communicating and promoting the vision, mission, values, goals and objectives of the organization
Week 5: Organizational Culture Establishing and maintaining an organizational culture that adheres to mission and values and promotes community inclusion for individuals with intellectual and developmental disabilities
Week 6: Organizational Change Presenting, supporting and implementing innovative practices to improve organizational services and practice and building a resilient organization
Week 7: Strategic Planning Directing staff and working with Board to effectively identify areas of future growth and development and developing a strategy for implementing a business plan
Week(s) 8 & 9: Communications (oral and written) Using strategies to enhance effective written oral and interpersonal communication skills
Week: 10: Governance Working with Board members to develop organizational policies to guide performance and accountability
Week(s) 11 & 12: Financial Management Managing utilization of resources and a system of internal controls; budgeting; and acquiring resources to support programmatic requirements
Semester 2: Leadership and Management 12 weeks, begins February 4, 2017
Week 1: Public Policy Advocating for public policy and social justice changes at local, state and national levels
Week 2: Leadership Styles Analyzing leadership styles using the DISC (dominance, influence, steadiness, conscientiousness) behavioral assessment instrument
Week 3: Risk Management and Legal Affairs Protecting agency from undue risk by assuring that policies and procedures exist in all areas; working with legal representatives
Week 4: Marketing and Public Relations Establishing and maintaining relationships with key organizational constituencies; using a variety of approaches to describe, promote and interest others in the organization; community relations
Week(s) 5 & 6: Coaching and Supporting Employees Using a variety of techniques such as motivating, directing, fostering growth, empowering, setting goals, supporting efforts, providing feedback, using problem solving and conflict resolution techniques and managing and supporting a team-based culture; and adhering to labor relations
Week 7: Managing Projects and Programs Supporting and assisting staff in securing funding and implementing programs to advance mission of organization; complying with federal and state rules and regulations, i.e., HIPPA, CMS, DDS, certification; contracting regulations and accountability
Week 8: Managing Diversity and Cross Cultural Understanding Employing a diverse workforce and promoting an open and sensitive workplace culture
Week 9: Information Technology Using technology resources to enhance the organization's processes and visibility
Week 10: Performance Management Developing systems for measuring performance, analyzing results and using these data to make strategic and operational decisions
Week 11: Collaboration Collaborating with other organizations to assess service needs and leverage resources to achieve mission
Week 12: Review and Wrap Up

Frequently Asked Questions

Who is the program tailored for?

The courses are designed for developmental disability (DD) agency professionals employed in upper and middle management positions who aspire to career progression.

What is the schedule for the program?

The length of the program is 24 weeks with a break over the holidays. The first semester begins the week of September 12, 2016 and ends the week of December 5, 2016. There are 12 weeks in the first semester and 12 weeks in the second semester, which will begin the week of February 13, 2017 and end the week of May 8, 2017.

The first three-hour session the week of September 12 will include a program orientation, as well as content, to be held on the University of Massachusetts Boston (UMB) campus.

There will be 10 online training courses following the first session. It is anticipated that each online module will take at least three hours to complete. The online courses can be completed at the time and place of the student's choice as long as the course is completed within the designated week. This gives working professionals flexibility in scheduling. There will be a final session in the first semester at UMB. The second semester will follow the same format with a face-to-face session to begin the semester followed by 10 weeks of online instruction and then conclude with a final session at UMB.

What are the pre-requisites for the program?

There are no academic requirements for the program.

What is the cost?

The cost for the program is $3,500, plus a book fee of $200-300 for each semester.

Payment can be made in full once accepted into the program. Otherwise, two separate payments of $1,750 each are allowed. First payment is due on or before August 31, 2016 and second payment is due on or before February 1, 2017.

Payment: Each application will be reviewed by the program’s staff. Upon approval, the student will be requested to make payment via credit card. Payment is due by August 11, 2016.

Payment will be made through credit card and due when participants register.

  12. Marketing and public relations

How was the curriculum determined?

University of Massachusetts Boston instructional staff conducted a curriculum review of other executive management and leadership programs offered around the country to ensure that the DD Agency Leadership and Management certificate curriculum reflected contemporary practices. Furthermore, four focus groups were held in Massachusetts attended by DD provider leaders and upper and middle managers to provide input into the competencies required to lead DD agencies now and into the future.

What is the application process?

Interested students must complete and submit an application form by July 15, 2016. A candidate's supervisor will be asked to sign the application allowing for students to implement policies and practices within their organizations based on their learning and through course field assignments.

When a student has been accepted into the program, they will be required to submit the fee for the first semester ($1,750) on or before August 31, 2016. Payment for the second semester ($1,750) will be due on or before February 1, 2017. Students will not receive a certificate of completion unless payment is made in full.

Who will teach courses?

There will be a combination of academic professors, instructors, and professional leaders serving within DD provider organizations and/or Massachusetts state agencies.

What happens if I fail?

Assignments are not graded. Instructors will provide continual support and feedback to students on field assignments until the student receives competency levels.

What are the requirements to receive the certificate?

Participation/attendance at four sessions at UMB and completion of online courses, webinars, and field assignments associated with courses are required to receive the certificate. Each student will receive a syllabus that will include learning objectives for each course, description of field assignments, and reading and resource lists.

Are there other requirements?

Students will be required to have access to Internet with either a Windows or Mac operating system. Technical assistance, free of charge, will be provided to students experiencing difficulty with the online training platform.

How many credits will I receive?

This is a non-credit program.

How does this program differ from other leadership and management training programs?

The DD Agency Leadership and Management certificate program has been developed specifically for individuals who aspire to be in leadership positions within DD organizations. The certificate program is unique to the field, which will better position graduates in leadership and management careers in the DD field.
